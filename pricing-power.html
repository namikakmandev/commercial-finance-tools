How to Read This

Pricing power = an industry's ability to raise prices faster than its input costs are rising.

When the spread is positive, the industry is expanding gross margins on average — output prices outpaced input prices over the window. When it's negative, the industry is absorbing input inflation faster than it can pass it through — margins are compressing.

Caveats: This measures industry averages, not individual firms. A well-managed company in a weak industry can still hold pricing power. Services industries also under-represent wage inflation since PPI input baskets focus on intermediate goods.

Türkiye numbers are approximations — TÜİK does not publish matched output/input pairs, so input PPI is derived as a weighted basket of upstream cost categories per industry.
